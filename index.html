The Levi Problem

The Levi Problem was the central problem in complex analysis in the first half of the 20th century. It boils down to proving that two classes of domain (connected open set) are equivalent:

  • the domains of holomorphy, which are those domains upon which there exists a holomorphic function that cannot extend beyond the boundary of the domain
  • the pseudoconvex domains, which are those with the property that if a sequence of closed unit balls map holomorphically into the domain, and the limit of the boundaries of these embedded balls lies in the domain, then the limit of the interiors of the balls also lies in the domain.
In the case of one complex variable this problem is trivial -- it is simple enough to show that every domain is both a domain of holomorphy and a pseudoconvex domain. As soon as we consider multiple variables, however, the situation becomes far more complicated.
