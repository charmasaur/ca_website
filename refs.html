The Levi Problem references

The history of the Levi problem dates back to the start of the 20th century, and is still of interest today. Here's a list of some important occurrences in that history.

  • Weierstrass 1876. Prove the Weierstrass factorization theorem, about constructing holomorphic functions with prescribed zeros.
  • Mittag-Leffler 1876. Proves the so-called Mittag-Leffler theorem, about constructing holomorphic functions with prescribed poles.
  • Cousin 1895. Introduces the Cousin problems, which ask for conditions under which holomorphic functions can be constructed with prescribed zeros and poles. These questions ask for generalisations of Mittag-Leffler's and Weierstrass's theorems.
  • Hartogs 1906. Demonstrates that there are domains on which every holomorphic function admits an extension to a larger domain, and therefore that the notion of a domain of holomorphy is non-trivial in the case of multiple complex variables.
  • Levi 1910.
  • Levi 1911
  • Cartan and Thullen 1932 (holomorphic convexity)
  • Lelong 1945 (plurisubharmonic functions)
  • Oka 1942
  • Oka 1953 (Riemann domains spread over C^n)
  • Grauert 1958 (complex manifolds)
  • Narasimhan 1961 (complex spaces)
