The Levi Problem references

The history of the Levi problem dates back to the start of the 20th century, and is still of interest today. Here's a list of some important occurrences in that history.

  • Cousin 1895. This paper introduces the so-called Cousin problems, which essentially ask for conditions under which holomorphic functions can be constructed with prescribed zeros and poles. The questions generalise those answered by the Mittag-Leffler Theorem and Weierstrass Factorization Theorem. Domains of holomorphy are tied intimitely to these problems.
  • Hartogs 1906 (holomorphic extension)
  • Levi 1910
  • Levi 1911
  • Cartan and Thullen 1932 (holomorphic convexity)
  • Lelong 1945 (plurisubharmonic functions)
  • Oka 1942
  • Oka 1953 (Riemann domains spread over C^n)
  • Grauert 1958 (complex manifolds)
  • Narasimhan 1961 (complex spaces)
